Software Overview

InDesign is a desktop publishing application for creating professional layouts for print and digital publishing, such as brochures, magazines, posters, reports, and interactive online documents. The software includes many advanced page layout tools, free stock templates, and collaboration features to sync work across teams.

In InDesign, you can begin from scratch or choose from one of their thousands of templates. You can customize each object as you design your document, including scaling images and text that InDesign automatically fits within document guidelines. While editing a document, the Live Preflight feature helps you fix page errors as they arise. InDesign also supports automated cross-references, conditional text, and bullets and numbering across large documents. In addition, you can create your own content or import data from a database in XML format.

InDesign includes advanced features for creating digital content, such as eBooks and digital magazines. The application allows you to add video, audio, animations, and slideshows to your documents to create a more interactive experience for your audience. And if you are editing a collaborative document, you can easily share it for review with team members and sync fonts and graphics across projects. When you finish designing your layout, you can publish your content directly to the web or in various file formats, such as .PDF, .EPUB, .JPEG, .PNG, .EPS, .HTML, and .XHTML.

Summary

InDesign is a professional print and digital publishing tool included with the Adobe Creative Cloud (CC) suite that you can also use as a standalone application. Its wide range of features will help you create professional-looking documents and digital designs, whether on your own or with a team. While it may be overwhelming for casual users, Adobe InDesign is an excellent option for professional publishers.
